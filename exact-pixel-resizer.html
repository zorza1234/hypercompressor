Why Do Portals Require Exact Pixels?

Government agencies, banking portals (IBPS), and academic boards (NTA, SSC, UPSC) rely on automated software to print millions of admit cards. If your uploaded photo or signature does not perfectly match the exact pixel grid built into their ID cards, the automated system will reject your application instantly. Common requirements are exactly 200 x 230 pixels for photos, or 140 x 60 pixels for signatures.

Frequently Asked Questions

My image looks slightly stretched. Is this okay?

Yes. When you uncheck the "Lock Aspect Ratio" box and force an image into rigid dimensions, it will stretch slightly. Portals accept this minor stretching as long as the pixel count perfectly matches their system requirements.
